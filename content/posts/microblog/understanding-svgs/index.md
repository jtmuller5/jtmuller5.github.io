The first official version of Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG 1.0) was released in 2001. 

Before this (and even after), a majority of the images on the internet were raster images. Raster images are made up of pixels, which means they don't scale well. If you try to scale a raster image up, it will become pixelated. If you try to scale it down, it will lose detail.
