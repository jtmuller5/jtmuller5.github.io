# November 19, 2024      

## A Hard Problem

It's easy to fool yourself into thinking superintelligenc is only a good training run away. Maybe if we scale something up enough we'll meet god. More data, more training time, more inference. Who knows.

But if you imagine the problem _as if it were_ a different problem, it starts to feel more and more difficult. For example, what if creating AGI was similar to solving autoimmune issues in human biology. That problem is multifaceted, layered, complex, context dependent, and filled with unknowns. Or what if the difficulty of creating AGI was equal to the difficulty of creating anti-gravity technology. Does it start to seem further away or closer?

Creating superintelligence is one of the hardest problems in the universe because it is really an attempt to replicate the creative force of the universe. To create a machine or system that can continuously learn and improve itself without limits is actually a much harder problem than it appears on the surface and I'd argue that any other technological challenge you can think of is easier. Creating anti-gravity tech. Solving mental disorders within the neuronal connectome. Inventing teleportation.
