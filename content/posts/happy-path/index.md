In the future, there won't be SMS and the web and native apps. They will all be combined into an experience that aims to be seamless. When you call an AI agent, you'll be able to interact with the conversation, see it transcribed in realtime, respond with your voice or your phone, and see what the agent thought you said. Your conversations and chat history will follow you from device to device without you needing to do anything. And everywhere you go, you'll be able to use your input of choice to command the AIs of the new world.

There are interactions today that are frustrating. Try to schedule an appointment after hours and you'll feel the rain. Try to contact a lawyer or a bank or a SaaS company and listen to your bones creak as you are ping ponged between agents and automated chat bots. There's something sickly about all of this. We live in a time where worldwide communication is instantaneous yet our methods of communication are ancient. A phone call is one dimensional. To keep pace with the AI minds of tomorrow, we need to add some layers to our communication.

Voice. Touch. Movement. Vision.

And we need better UI to illustrate how our inputs are affecting the AIs we chat with. We should see realtime transcripts. We should see realtime options for any question the AI asks. We should have control over how the AI interprets us.

And finally, our preferences across interactions should be preserved so we never have to say or type our address more than once if it hasn't changed. Your phone number should be connected to your secure data and you should be able to give AI permission to view it when necessary.

Where do we talk to AIs the most? Is it only during a scheduling call? And if it is, will it be that way in 5 years? Or will we do more of everything we do through the lens of AI?

The issue is making this new filtered communication better and faster, not the opposite. What is a small project I can work on to test this?
