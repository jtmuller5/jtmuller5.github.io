What is Hermitian?

A [Hermitian matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermitian_matrix) is a complex square matrix that is equal to its own conjugate transpose.

What is a conjugate transpose?

A [conjugate transpose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugate_transpose), or adjoint matrix, is the matrix obtained by transposing *A* and applying complex conjugation on each entry.

What is complex conjugation?

In mathematics, the [complex conjugate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complex_conjugate) of a complex number is the number with an equal real part and an imaginary part equal in magnitude but opposite in sign. Complex conjugates can be illustrated via an Argand diagram.

What is an Argand diagram?

An Argand diagram is a geometric plot of complex numbers as points where the x-axis is the real axis and the y-axis is the imaginary axis. In an Argand diagram, the complex conjugates appear as a reflections on either side of the real axis.
